Old vs New Tax Regime
Which one actually saves you money?

Every year, millions of Indians pay more tax than they legally need to — simply because they never compared the two regimes side by side. Their HR never cared and their CAs are under huge burden to look beyond the filing process. Nobody showed them the numbers. This article does exactly that, with a free calculator at the end so you can find out for your specific situation in under 3 minutes.

ITR Filing Deadline: July 31, 2026
This is the last date for individual taxpayers to file their Income Tax Return for FY 2025-26. Miss it and you pay a late fee of ₹5,000. More importantly, you might also miss the chance to pick the right regime and overpay tax unnecessarily.
The two rulebooks

Old Regime vs New Regime: what is the difference?

Every year the government asks you to pick one of two tax systems. Think of them as two different deals on the table. You pick one, you live by its rules for that year.

Old Regime
Higher rates, but lots of discounts
You pay higher tax rates, but the government lets you reduce your income first by showing investments, rent, insurance, and home loan payments. Plan well, pay less.
Your parents used this Rewards planners More paperwork
New Regime
Lower rates, but no discounts
The government offers you lower slab rates upfront. The trade-off: almost no deductions allowed. What you earn is what gets taxed, minus a flat ₹75,000 standard deduction.
Default since FY 2023-24 Zero tax up to ₹12L No investment proofs

What the new regime takes away

Deductions you lose when you go New Regime

These deductions exist only in the old regime. Each one legally reduces how much of your income gets taxed, but only if you have planned for them.

The magic breakeven number
If your total deductions (HRA + 80C + home loan interest + 80D + NPS) cross ₹3,75,000 per year, the Old Regime almost always wins for incomes between ₹10L and ₹20L. Below that threshold, New Regime wins on both lower rates and simplicity.

ITR-1 (Sahaj): For simple salaried taxpayers
You qualify if your income is from one employer, one house property, and bank interest — and total income does not exceed ₹50 lakh. This is the simplest form and takes under 15 minutes to file online.

Salaried + capital gains
Sold stocks or MFs, multiple properties, income above ₹50L
⌄
ITR-2: Salaried with capital gains or multiple properties
Use ITR-2 if you sold stocks, mutual funds, or property during the year, have income from more than one house, or if total income exceeds ₹50 lakh. You cannot use ITR-1 if you traded shares or redeemed mutual funds.
Salaried person who sold ELSS or stocks Person with a flat on rent plus salary NRI with Indian income Income above ₹50 lakh

ITR-3: Regular business or professional income
If you run a business or practice a profession with detailed books of accounts, ITR-3 is for you. This applies when business income is too large for the presumptive scheme or when you voluntarily opt out.
Doctor with a clinic and billing records CA or lawyer with a firm Business owner above ₹50L gross receipts Opted out of presumptive scheme last year

This is your form if you earn independently without being an employee and opt for presumptive taxation (44ADA for professionals, 44AD for small business). Declare a flat percentage of receipts as profit — no detailed books needed. Use the Freelancer calculator tab below to estimate your tax.

Action checklist

What to do this week

1
Run the calculator below with your actual numbers: salary, HRA, rent, 80C, and home loan. Takes 3 minutes.
2
If New Regime wins, you are likely already on it (it is the default). Just file your ITR before July 31. No proofs needed.
3
If Old Regime wins, inform your employer now if TDS has not been finalized. Collect all investment proofs: 80C, health insurance, rent receipts, home loan interest certificate.
4
Salaried employees can correct their regime choice at ITR filing time even if they declared differently to HR earlier in the year.
5
Do not let your CA decide without explaining why. Ask them to show you the numbers for both regimes. It is your legal right and your money.

    Section 44ADA / 44AD: Presumptive Taxation in plain words. If you earn from a profession or small business independently (not as an employee), the government lets you declare a fixed percentage of your gross receipts as profit without maintaining detailed books. Tax is calculated on that declared profit. A CA is still recommended for final filing — but this calculator helps you understand your liability before you go.
